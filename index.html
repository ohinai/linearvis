How to read it

m, the slope, rotates the line. The pink dashed segment marks the rise (vertical change) and the teal dashed segment marks the run (horizontal change). The slope is the ratio: for every 1 step right, the line climbs m units. Positive m tilts up, negative tilts down, and zero gives you a flat horizontal line.

b, the y-intercept, slides the entire line up or down without changing its angle. The amber dot marks where the line crosses the y-axis — always at the point (0, b). That's because plugging x = 0 into y = mx + b leaves just y = b.

Why this form is useful

Slope-intercept form bundles the two things you most want to know about a line into a single readable expression. Given any equation in this shape, you can sketch the line in seconds: start at (0, b), then step over 1 and up m, over 1 and up m again, and you've got it.
